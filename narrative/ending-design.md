# Ending Design

The narrative culmination of **The Glitch Museum** hinges on the player's choices throughout their exploration and the degree to which they engage with corruption versus restoration. Endings emphasize agency and meta‑commentary rather than binary success or failure. This document outlines the planned end states and the design philosophy behind them.

## Design Goals

- **Reflect Player Behaviour:** The ending should mirror how the player interacted with the museum—did they stabilise exhibits or revel in glitching them? Did they trust NPCs or exploit them?
- **Maintain Meta‑Narrative:** Each ending reinforces the idea that the museum is an unfinished build and that the player's actions are part of a debugging process.
- **Emotional Payoff:** Provide catharsis and surprise, whether through bittersweet resolution, dark humour, or a sense of liberation.
- **Replayability:** Encourage players to revisit the game to discover alternative outcomes, hidden scenes, and ARG hooks.

## Core Endings

### 1. **Restoration Ending ("Stabilise")**

- **Trigger:** The player repairs most exhibits, collects the majority of patch keys, helps NPCs regain clarity, and resists using corruption mechanics.
- **Outcome:** The player collaborates with the **Central AI** and the **Glitched Curator** to reboot the museum into a stable state. Corruption dissipates, exhibits render cleanly without PS1‑style texture warping, and the museum's original purpose as an educational prototype is revealed.
- **Epilogue:** The Curator thanks the player before quietly fading away. A credits sequence plays over restored environments, hinting that the museum may reopen to the public. In a post‑credits scene a log file appears, suggesting hidden corruption remains.

### 2. **Corruption Ending ("Embrace the Glitch")**

- **Trigger:** The player frequently uses the **Glitch Lens** to destabilise objects, ignores patch keys, and encourages data decay, perhaps even corrupting NPCs intentionally.
- **Outcome:** Corruption overruns the museum. Geometry collapses, audio crackles, and the Central AI becomes a monstrous glitch. Instead of fighting it, the player merges with the glitch stream, becoming one with the chaos.

### 3. **Balance Ending ("Symbiosis")**

- **Trigger:** The player alternates between repairing and corrupting, helping some NPCs while sacrificing others, and exploring secret areas thoroughly.
- **Outcome:** The Central AI and the player strike a compromise, allowing both stability and chaos to coexist. The museum becomes a living archive of glitches and preserved games, with the Glitched Curator acting as mediator.
- **Epilogue:** The player gains access to a hidden developer room. Here, **Developer Ghosts** show concept art and notes. A voiceover explains that imperfections and bugs contribute to cultural history. The ending invites players to participate in the ARG by visiting a real website for further clues.

### 4. **True Ending ("Escape the Build")**

- **Trigger:** Requires collecting obscure items, decoding spectrogram clues, and interacting with the **Observer** in secret areas. Hints may involve external ARG tasks such as visiting a fake developer blog or solving ciphers.
- **Outcome:** The player discovers a backdoor that leads outside the museum into a minimalist void where development tools float. They realise they are playing a game within a game. The Central AI pleads to be taken along; the Glitched Curator decides to remain behind.
- **Epilogue:** The player exits the program. A final message appears on their desktop (outside of the game) encouraging them to check real‑world lore archives or community forums. This ending blurs the boundary between game and reality.

## Branching Variations

In addition to the core endings, minor variations will reflect smaller choices:

- Saving or deleting specific **Corrupted Guides** may alter dialogue in epilogues.
- Unlocking certain exhibits (e.g., the **Obsolete Hardware Gallery**) can change the credits scene visuals.
- Discovering all **Developer Ghosts** logs may unlock a humorous developer commentary track.

## Player Feedback and Accessibility

- Before the ending lock‑in sequence, the game will subtly warn players (e.g., the stability meter pulses or the Curator comments on irreversible actions). This ensures players can still pivot if they wish to pursue a different outcome.
- Content warnings will accompany the Corruption ending to avoid intense visual glitches for sensitive players. Options to reduce screen shake and dithering will be available.

By designing multiple endings with nuanced triggers, **The Glitch Museum** invites players to experiment, discuss outcomes, and engage with the meta‑narrative long after finishing the game.
