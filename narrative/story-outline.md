# Story Outline

## Premise
The player awakens in a virtual museum curated from the remnants of lost and unfinished software. Guided only by a mysterious Glitch Lens, they explore exhibits of scrapped games, corrupted files, rogue AI experiments and internet artifacts.

## Act I – Arrival
- The museum lobby introduces the hub structure and basic mechanics. Players meet the **Glitched Curator**, an NPC who seems helpful but occasionally malfunctions.
- Early objectives involve repairing the lobby using patch keys and learning to use the lens to reveal hidden developer notes.

## Act II – Discovery
- As players venture into the wings, they encounter more severe corruption and begin finding **Developer Ghosts** – AI that are echoes of the original programmers. These ghosts drop hints about the museum's true purpose.
- Anomalous puzzles suggest that the museum isn't just a showcase but a **sandbox used during development** of an unfinished game.

## Act III – Realisation
- Pieces of a hidden build manifest: commented-out code becomes physical pathways, test levels appear behind exhibits, and patch keys unlock a **Developer Archive** wing where confidential logs reveal the project's cancellation.
- The Glitched Curator becomes increasingly unstable, hinting at its awareness of being trapped in an incomplete game.

## Act IV – Confrontation
- The player discovers a control room with access to the game's source tree. They confront the primary AI, possibly the **Director**, who is torn between preserving the museum and escaping deletion.
- Choices revolve around stabilising the museum (applying all patches) or embracing instability to access a hidden "void" where cut content resides.

## Act V – Ending
The conclusion depends on the player's actions:
- **Stabilise**: The museum is preserved as a archival memory. NPCs regain clarity, but the meta narrative implies the player remains inside a curated artifact.
- **Corrupt**: The player dives into the unfinished void, uncovering the raw tools and ending up in a bizarre credits sequence that breaks the fourth wall.
- **Balance**: A mixed path reveals that the player themselves is part of the debugging toolset, blurring lines between user and developer.

This outline leaves room for player agency while supporting the core twist: the museum itself is a game that never shipped.
