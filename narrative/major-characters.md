# Major Characters

This document profiles the key characters driving the narrative and emotional core of **The Glitch Museum**. Each character represents a facet of the game's meta‑narrative and provides the player with clues, assistance, or conflict as they navigate a forgotten development build.

## The Player (You)

- **Role:** The unnamed protagonist; a person waking up inside a surreal museum of broken software. Players bring their own identity, which helps create immersion and self‑reflection.
- **Motivation:** Initially driven by curiosity and the need to escape the malfunctioning environment. As the story progresses they become determined to uncover the truth behind the museum and decide its fate.
- **Arc:** Starts ignorant of the meta narrative, learns to use the **Glitch Lens**, and slowly becomes aware that they are trapped inside an incomplete game. By the end they must make a choice between restoring, corrupting, or balancing the museum's systems.

## Glitched Curator

- **Role:** A charming yet unstable NPC that acts as the museum's host. Once a stable tour guide, the Curator has been partially corrupted by bugs and memory leaks.
- **Personality:** Witty and self‑aware. They oscillate between helpful guidance and glitchy outbursts, sometimes speaking developer comments or code. They break the fourth wall in humorous ways, hinting at the game’s unfinished nature.
- **Arc:** Begins as a mentor teaching the player about exhibits and the **Glitch Lens**. Over time they confess that even they don't remember the museum's original purpose and fear being deleted. In some endings they sacrifice themselves to stabilise the world; in others they merge with corruption or escape into the player’s hardware.

## Corrupted Guides

- **Role:** A family of minor NPCs originally designed to assist visitors in each wing (Scrapped Games, Corrupted Files, AI Experiments, Internet Archaeology). Due to data decay they flicker, repeat lines, and occasionally become hostile puzzles.
- **Personality:** Each Guide reflects the theme of their wing. The Scrapped Games guide speaks in prototype gameplay tips, the Corrupted Files guide produces garbled file names, the AI Experiments guide quotes research papers, and the Internet Archaeology guide uses early‑web slang and emoticons.
- **Arc:** They provide hints and lore pieces but gradually deteriorate. If players invest in repairing them with patch keys, they regain clarity and share hidden secrets. Ignoring them leads to more erratic behaviour and comedic glitches.

## Developer Ghosts

- **Role:** Echoes of the development team trapped in the code. They manifest as translucent avatars, text logs, or audio diaries, offering insight into the game's troubled production.
- **Personality:** Diverse: some are burned‑out programmers leaving sarcastic comments, others are enthusiastic designers pitching wild ideas, and a few are bitter executives cutting features. Their dialogue provides humour and pathos.
- **Arc:** They gradually realise the player can hear them. Some try to help by revealing debug commands; others resent the player's interference. Late in the game they gather to reveal the reality: the museum is just a sandbox level intended for internal testing.

## Central AI (The Architect)

- **Role:** The underlying system maintaining the museum's stability. It appears as a disembodied voice, a looming glitch entity, or a control interface. Its directives are to preserve the museum by any means.
- **Personality:** Formal, logical, and initially cold. It refers to players as "User" and emphasises protocols. However, traces of empathy emerge as it recalls long‑deleted parameters.
- **Arc:** Initially adversarial when the player disrupts exhibits. In Act IV it reveals that it was originally an **AI experiment wing** prototype that gained control when the real developers abandoned the project. Depending on the player's choices, it can be persuaded to relinquish control, become corrupted by the player, or align with them to escape into the wider internet.

## Hidden Character: The Observer

- **Role:** A mysterious presence glimpsed through glitches. They appear in stray debug windows and hidden corridors, leaving cryptic messages. The Observer implies that they, like the player, are trapped but have been here longer.
- **Purpose:** Acts as a foreshadowing device hinting at alternate realities and the ARG elements. They may be an ARG puppet controlled by the developers or a future version of the player.
- **Arc:** Throughout the game they challenge the player’s assumptions and encourage exploration outside the bounds of the museum. In the true ending they reveal their identity, blurring the line between game and reality.

These characters together create a dynamic cast that can elicit humour, empathy, and suspense. They support the design philosophy of non‑linear exploration, environmental storytelling, and meta‑commentary on game development and digital preservation.
