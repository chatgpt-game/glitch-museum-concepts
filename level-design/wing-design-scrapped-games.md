# Wing Design – Scrapped Games

The Scrapped Games wing is a melancholic gallery of unfinished prototypes and cancelled experiments. This space is deliberately disjointed, presenting broken levels and incomplete gameplay mechanics that players must navigate using the Glitch Lens. The wing emphasises environmental storytelling, revealing the creative process behind games that never made it to release.

## Layout

- **Fragmented Exhibits:** Each room represents a different abandoned project, complete with developer notes and incomplete assets. Hallways connect these exhibits in a non‑linear fashion.
- **Hub Atrium:** A central atrium serves as a waypoint, allowing players to revisit exhibits after they obtain new patch keys.
- **Debug Maze:** Hidden behind a glitched wall, the wing contains a developer “debug room” that doubles as a puzzle maze.

## Gameplay and Puzzles

- **Prototype Puzzles:** Players interact with unfinished mechanics—broken physics, missing scripts and placeholder objects—and must stabilise them using patch keys.
- **Lost Source Hunt:** Bits of code or asset fragments are scattered across the exhibits. Collecting these fragments unlocks a more complete version of a scrapped mini‑game.
- **Temporal Layers:** Switching between early and late builds with the Glitch Lens reveals changes in level geometry and narrative context.

## Aesthetic & Atmosphere

The visual style draws on early 3D prototypes and paper mock‑ups. Expect untextured polygons, developer annotations and glitchy audio cues. Lighting is harsh and flat, evoking the feel of a build still in grey‑box testing.

## Patch Keys & Progression

Finding patch keys within prototypes slowly restores systems in this wing. Each key might repair a doorway, spawn an NPC or unlock a new exhibit in the atrium. As the wing stabilises, more narrative context about why each game was scrapped becomes available.

## Secrets & Hidden Areas

Curious players can uncover:

- **Developer Commentary Rooms:** Spaces where the original developers recorded voice notes, concept art and diaries.
- **Recyclable Assets Vault:** A hidden cache of models and textures intended for reuse in other projects.
- **Meta Glitches:** Rare anomalies that break the fourth wall, hinting at the larger mystery of the museum’s existence.
