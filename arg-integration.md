# ARG Integration

An Alternate Reality Game (ARG) can extend the Glitch Museum beyond its digital walls, engaging players through real-world websites, social media, and hidden clues. This document proposes approaches to integrate ARG elements into the core experience.

## Goals

- Enhance immersion by blurring the line between game and reality.
- Encourage community collaboration and sharing of discoveries.
- Provide additional lore and backstory for players who wish to dive deeper.
- Maintain optionality; ARG participation should enrich the experience but not be required to complete the main game.

## Possible ARG Components

- **Fake Developer Websites**  
  Create websites for the fictional development studio or museum board. Hide clues in the HTML source, comment strings, or hidden pages. Example: a “Careers” page that lists positions for “Debugging Reality Engineer.”

- **Social Media Accounts**  
  Run Twitter, Mastodon, or Bluesky accounts for in‑game characters (e.g., the Glitched Curator). They can post cryptic updates, glitchy images, or coordinate “maintenance schedules.” Followers might receive direct messages with puzzle hints.

- **Email Subscriptions**  
  Allow players to sign up for “patch notes” newsletters. Emails could include patch key codes or ASCII art that, when decoded, point to secret museum rooms.

- **Hidden Codes in Audio/Video**  
  Include spectrogram images in soundtrack files or QR codes in promotional videos. Scanning reveals real URLs or passcodes used in the game.

- **Physical Mailings**  
  For dedicated fans, send tangible artifacts such as floppy disks, printed circuit boards, or postcards with cryptic messages. The contents could reference real-world coordinates or phone numbers.

## Integration Points within the Game

- **Terminals & Kiosks**  
  In-game terminals can display login prompts that require credentials discovered via ARG clues. Successful logins unlock secret exhibits or lore entries.

- **Developer Ghost Hints**  
  Ghost NPCs can break the fourth wall, whispering website URLs or anagrams of Twitter handles.

- **Achievements & Rewards**  
  Completing ARG milestones could unlock cosmetic items (e.g., alternate lens skins) or unique music tracks.

## Management Considerations

- **Scalability**  
  Plan puzzles that can be solved by individuals or large groups. Avoid requiring players in specific geographic locations unless there are alternative routes.

- **Monitoring & Moderation**  
  Monitor ARG channels to provide hints if the community gets stuck and to maintain narrative consistency. Enforce community guidelines to keep interactions safe and inclusive.

- **Lifecycle**  
  Decide whether ARG content will be ongoing or time‑limited. Provide archives for players who join after certain phases are complete.

## Ethical & Safety Concerns

- Avoid encouraging trespassing or dangerous real-world activities.
- Keep personal data collection minimal and transparent.
- Clearly distinguish official ARG channels to protect players from scams.

## Future Expansions

- Collaborate with other indie games to create crossover ARG puzzles.
- Introduce a live‑acted event (e.g., a streamed “developer conference”) where real actors play in-game characters and respond to community chat.
