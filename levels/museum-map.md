# Museum Map

The **Glitch Museum** is a labyrinthine digital institution built as a hub world for players to explore. It comprises a central lobby with multiple wings, each dedicated to a different category of digital artifact. The intention is to encourage non linear exploration while giving players a sense of progression as they collect "patch keys" to stabilize areas.

## Overall Layout

- **Central Hub – Lobby**  
  The lobby is the player's starting area. It contains:
  - An information desk manned by the glitchy Curator NPC.
  - Display cases with teasers of each wing.
  - A central elevator shaft or staircase leading down to deeper levels that unlock later in the game.
  - A patch key kiosk where players slot recovered keys to repair new wings.

- **Wings**  
  Four primary wings radiate from the hub:
  1. **Scrapped Games Wing** – Exhibits prototypes, betas, and unreleased games. Areas feel unfinished with missing textures and placeholder geometry. Puzzles involve reconstructing missing assets.
  2. **Corrupted Files Wing** – Focuses on corrupted save files, unstable software, and broken hardware. Expect heavy environmental corruption: shifting geometry, static-filled visuals, and audio glitches. The wing houses data reconstruction puzzles.
  3. **AI Experiments Wing** – Contains AI companions gone rogue, chatbots, and neural net art. NPCs here respond to the player's Glitch Lens and will comment on being observed. Some puzzles involve training AIs with limited inputs.
  4. **Internet Archaeology Wing** – An archive of early web pages, forums, and online communities. The environment resembles a 3D browser window. Players navigate through geo-cities‑style neighborhoods and piece together fragments of lost internet history.

- **Secret & Bonus Areas**  
  - **Developer Archives** – Hidden behind breakable walls or accessible via vents. Contains developer notes, patch logs, and early prototypes.
  - **Obsolete Hardware Gallery** – Displays retro consoles and PC hardware, each with its own micro‑puzzle.
  - **The Void** – A black‑and‑white void representing deleted content. Accessible only after collecting all patch keys.

## Navigation & Gating

- **Patch Keys**  
  Each wing requires a patch key to stabilize the entrance. Keys are obtained by solving major puzzles in other wings. This encourages players to revisit earlier areas with new tools.
- **Glitch Lens Upgrades**  
  Additional lens functions (e.g., property swapping) are tied to exploration. The museum map indicates which wings need certain upgrades.
- **Environmental Puzzles**  
  Corridors might be blocked by corrupted geometry or time loops. Players solve small puzzles to progress deeper into a wing.

## Aesthetic & Atmosphere

- **PS1‑Era Scale**  
  Hallways are wide and low‑poly; textures are grainy and pixelated. Lighting is static but occasionally flickers due to glitches.
- **Signage**  
  Each wing is marked with signage made from ASCII art or CRT‑style fonts, adding to the retro feel. Signs occasionally flicker or display developer placeholder text.
- **Ambient Storytelling**  
  Items such as broken kiosks, scattered developer notes, and silent NPCs hint at the museum’s abandonment. Through the map layout, players sense that certain wings were closed abruptly.

## Future Extensions

- Additional wings could be added as expansions. Examples:
  - **Lost Media** – Focus on cancelled TV shows and unreleased music.
  - **AI Ethics** – Explore training data controversies through interactive exhibits.
  - **Modder’s Gallery** – Showcases fan mods and invites player‑created content.
